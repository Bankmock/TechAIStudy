Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What's the difference between a mile and a nautical mile?

A standard statute mile is 5,280 feet (1,609.344 meters) and is used for land measurement. A nautical mile is specifically used in maritime and aviation for navigation and is defined as 1,852 meters (approximately 1.15 statute miles). It's based on the Earth's circumference, being one minute of latitude.

Why are there so many units for length?

Different units developed historically in various cultures (e.g., based on body parts or everyday objects). While the metric system (SI) provides a universal standard, imperial units (like inches, feet, miles) remain in common use in some countries, particularly the United States. Specialized fields also developed units for their specific scales, like nanometers for atomic physics or light-years for astronomy.
