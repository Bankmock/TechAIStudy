Common Angle Conversion Formulas

Here are the underlying formulas used in angle conversions, illustrating the relationships between different units:

  • Degrees to Radians: $Radians = Degrees \times (\frac{\pi}{180})$
  • Radians to Degrees: $Degrees = Radians \times (\frac{180}{\pi})$
  • Degrees to Gradians: $Gradians = Degrees \times (\frac{10}{9})$
  • Gradians to Degrees: $Degrees = Gradians \times (\frac{9}{10})$
  • DMS to Decimal Degrees: $Decimal Degrees = Degrees + (\frac{Minutes}{60}) + (\frac{Seconds}{3600})$
  • Decimal Degrees to DMS:
    • Degrees = Integer part of Decimal Degrees
    • Minutes = Integer part of $((Decimal Degrees - Degrees) \times 60)$
    • Seconds = $(( (Decimal Degrees - Degrees) \times 60) - Minutes) \times 60$

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is DMS angle conversion?

DMS stands for Degrees, Minutes, Seconds. It's a way of expressing angles (or geographic coordinates like latitude and longitude) using a sexagesimal (base-60) system, where 1 degree = 60 minutes and 1 minute = 60 seconds.

Can I convert negative angles?

Yes, our angle converter supports both positive and negative angle values for all unit conversions.

Is this angle converter free to use?

Do I need to download any software?

No, this is a web-based tool. You can access and use our angle converter directly from your browser on any device.

What is the difference between radians and degrees?

Degrees divide a circle into 360 parts, while radians relate the angle to the arc length of a circle's radius. A full circle is 360 degrees or $2\pi$ radians.

Why are gradians used?

Gradians (or gons) are primarily used in surveying and some engineering fields, particularly in countries that adopted the metric system, as a right angle is exactly 100 gradians, simplifying calculations.
