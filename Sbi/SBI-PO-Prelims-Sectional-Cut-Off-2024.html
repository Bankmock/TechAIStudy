The SBI PO Prelims 2024 sectional cut-off is an important benchmark for candidates aspiring to secure one of the most prestigious jobs in India’s banking sector. Every year, the State Bank of India conducts the Probationary Officer (PO) recruitment exam in three phases: Prelims, Mains, and Interview. To move to the Mains stage, candidates must clear the sectional cut-off in the Prelims examination. In this post, we'll delve deep into the 2024 sectional cut-off, including category-wise details, factors influencing the cut-off, and preparation tips to surpass these cut-off marks.

What is the SBI PO Prelims Sectional Cut-Off?

The sectional cut-off in the SBI PO Prelims refers to the minimum score required in each individual section of the exam—namely English Language, Reasoning Ability, and Quantitative Aptitude. To qualify for the next round, a candidate must not only meet the overall cut-off but also clear each of these sectional cut-offs.

This is critical because even if your overall score is high, failing to meet the minimum required marks in any section will disqualify you from advancing to the Mains.

Key Factors Affecting the SBI PO Prelims Cut-Off

The following factors have a direct impact on the sectional and overall cut-offs for the SBI PO Prelims:

  • Number of Applicants: A higher number of candidates typically results in higher cut-offs due to increased competition.
  • Difficulty Level of the Exam: If the exam paper is particularly challenging in a given year, the cut-offs may be lower.
  • Total Number of Vacancies: Fewer available positions often lead to higher cut-offs.
  • Normalization Process: SBI normalizes scores to ensure fairness across different shifts if the exam is conducted in multiple sessions.

It’s essential to keep an eye on these factors to gauge how much you need to score to safely qualify for the Mains.

Preparation Tips to Clear the SBI PO Prelims Sectional Cut-Off

Here are some strategic tips to help you clear the sectional cut-offs with ease:

  1. Master the Basics: Make sure you have a strong foundation in English, Reasoning, and Quantitative Aptitude. Focus on improving your speed and accuracy in each section.
  2. Take Mock Tests: Regular mock tests help you simulate the real exam environment and identify your weak areas.
  3. Time Management: During the exam, allocate your time wisely between sections. Don’t spend too much time on one section at the expense of others.
  4. Analyze Previous Years' Papers: Go through previous years' question papers to understand the types of questions asked and the difficulty level.
  5. Sectional Focus: Prepare for each section separately to ensure you meet the sectional cut-off. For example, if your weak point is Quantitative Aptitude, devote more time to practicing problems from this section.

SBI PO Prelims Sectional Cut-Off 2024 - FAQs

Q1: What is the SBI PO Prelims sectional cut-off?

Answer: The sectional cut-off is the minimum score a candidate must achieve in each section (English Language, Quantitative Aptitude, and Reasoning Ability) to qualify for the SBI PO Mains examination. Failing to meet the sectional cut-off in any subject will disqualify the candidate from advancing to the next round.

Q2: What was the sectional cut-off for General category in 2024?

Answer: For the General category in 2024, the sectional cut-off for SBI PO Prelims was as follows:
- English: 8.75 marks
- Quantitative Aptitude: 9.00 marks
- Reasoning Ability: 10.00 marks
The overall cut-off was 59.25 marks.

Q3: What are the factors that affect the SBI PO cut-off?

Answer: Several factors influence the cut-off for the SBI PO exam, such as:
- The difficulty level of the exam
- The number of candidates appearing for the exam
- The number of vacancies available
- Previous years' cut-off trends.

Q4: How is the cut-off for SBI PO Mains different from Prelims?

Answer: The cut-off for SBI PO Mains is generally higher and includes additional factors like descriptive paper scores and performance in the interview phase. The Prelims cut-off is used to shortlist candidates for the Mains exam, while the Mains cut-off leads to the interview stage.

Q5: Where can I check the official cut-off marks?

Answer: The official cut-off marks are usually released on the State Bank of India's official website after each stage of the examination. You can visit SBI Careers to get the latest updates on cut-off scores.
