What is a GPA & CGPA Calculator Tool?

A **GPA (Grade Point Average) & CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) calculator** is an essential online tool for students to accurately assess their academic performance. It allows you to compute:

  • **GPA:** Your average grade points for a single academic period, such as a semester or term.
  • **CGPA:** Your overall average grade points accumulated across all completed academic periods.

Our **free online GPA/CGPA calculator** simplifies the complex process of converting letter grades and credit hours into a single numerical score. This tool is invaluable for monitoring your progress, setting academic goals, applying for scholarships, or preparing for higher studies.

Key advantages: Calculates both GPA and CGPA, supports multiple courses, instant results, helps track academic standing, and completely free to use.

Understanding GPA and CGPA Components

Key terms you need to know for accurate calculations:

Grade Points

Numerical value assigned to each letter grade (e.g., A=4.0, B=3.0, C=2.0).

Credit Hours

Weight of a course, typically representing the amount of classroom or study time.

Total Grade Points

Total Credit Hours

Formulas for GPA and CGPA

The core calculations are based on simple, yet crucial, formulas:

  • **GPA Formula:**
    $GPA = \frac{\text{Total Grade Points Earned in Semester}}{\text{Total Credit Hours Attempted in Semester}}$
    Example: (Course1 Grade Points * Course1 Credits) + (Course2 Grade Points * Course2 Credits) / (Course1 Credits + Course2 Credits)
  • **CGPA Formula:**
    $CGPA = \frac{\text{Cumulative Total Grade Points Earned}}{\text{Cumulative Total Credit Hours Attempted}}$
    This means summing up all grade points from all semesters and dividing by the sum of all credit hours from all semesters.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why is GPA/CGPA important?

GPA/CGPA is a standardized metric used by educational institutions, scholarship committees, and potential employers to quickly assess a student's academic performance and consistency. A strong GPA/CGPA can open doors to higher education, scholarships, internships, and job opportunities.

What if my university uses a different grading scale (e.g., 5.0 or 10.0 scale)?

Our calculator primarily uses the 4.0 scale. If your university uses a different scale, you will need to manually input the corresponding grade points for each letter grade according to your institution's specific system. The calculation logic (total grade points / total credits) will remain the same.

Does this calculator factor in failed courses or withdrawn courses?

For GPA/CGPA, usually, **failed courses (F grades)** are included and count as 0 grade points, which negatively impacts your average. **Withdrawn courses (W)** typically do not count towards your GPA/CGPA, as they don't have associated grade points. Always confirm your institution's specific policy on such courses.

Is my academic information saved or tracked?

No, any course details or grade information you enter into the calculator are processed only in your web browser. We do not store or track any of your personal or academic data, ensuring complete privacy and security.

Can I calculate a "target GPA" to see what grades I need?

While this specific calculator gives you your current GPA/CGPA, you can use it to estimate. For example, if you know your current CGPA and total credits, you can input hypothetical grades for upcoming courses to see how they would affect your overall CGPA, helping you set targets.
