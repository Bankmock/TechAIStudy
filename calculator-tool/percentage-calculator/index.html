Percentage Formulas Explained

Understanding the basic formulas behind percentages can help you grasp the results better:

  • How to find X% of Y:

    $Result = \frac{X}{100} \times Y$

    Example: 20% of 150 = $(20/100) \times 150 = 30$

  • How to find X is what percent of Y:

    $Result = \frac{X}{Y} \times 100\%$

    Example: 25 is what percent of 100? $(25/100) \times 100\% = 25\%$

  • How to calculate Percentage Change:

    $Percentage\ Change = \frac{\text{New Value} - \text{Old Value}}{\text{Old Value}} \times 100\%$

    Example: From 50 to 60. Percentage Change = $(60-50)/50 \times 100\% = 20\%$ increase.

Our calculator automates these formulas, providing instant and accurate answers.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What does "percentage" mean?

Percentage means "per hundred" or "out of one hundred." It's a way to express a number as a fraction of 100, often denoted by the percent sign (%).

Can I use this calculator for negative numbers?

Yes, our percentage calculator can handle negative numbers for 'X% of Y' and 'X is what percent of Y' calculations. For percentage change, it will correctly show increase or decrease even with negative values.

Is there a limit to the size of numbers I can enter?

Our calculator supports very large numbers, accommodating most practical percentage calculations you might need.

What if I need to add or subtract a percentage from a number?

You can use the 'X% of Y' function. First, calculate the percentage amount, then manually add or subtract it from the original number. For example, to add 10% to 100, calculate 10% of 100 (which is 10), then add it to 100 (100 + 10 = 110).

Is my input data saved or tracked?

No, your input numbers are not saved or tracked by our servers. All calculations are performed instantly on your local device for maximum privacy and security.

Why are percentages important in daily life?

Percentages help us understand proportions, changes, and comparisons easily. From understanding sales discounts, calculating grades, interpreting financial reports, to even understanding population growth, percentages are fundamental in making sense of data.
