Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How often are the exchange rates updated?

Our exchange rates are updated frequently, often every few minutes, to reflect the dynamic nature of the global foreign exchange market. This ensures you're always getting very close to live, real-time rates.

What is an interbank rate, and why is it different from retail rates?

The interbank rate is the wholesale exchange rate at which banks trade currencies with each other. Retail rates, which you get from banks, exchange bureaus, or credit card companies, include a markup or commission. This markup covers their operational costs and profit margins, making them slightly less favorable than the interbank rate you see here.

Can I use this for future exchange rate predictions?

No, our currency converter provides current, live exchange rates. It does not offer predictive capabilities for future rate movements, as currency markets are highly volatile and influenced by many factors.

Is my financial information secure with this tool?

Absolutely. Our currency converter only takes numerical amounts and currency selections. No personal financial information, account details, or sensitive data is ever requested, transmitted, or stored. All conversions happen directly in your browser.

Why do exchange rates fluctuate?

Exchange rates fluctuate due to various economic and geopolitical factors, including interest rates, inflation, political stability, economic performance, supply and demand for currencies, and speculation in the foreign exchange market.
